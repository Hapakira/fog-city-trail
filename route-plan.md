# Fog City Trail — Draft Route Plan

A end-to-end Pacifica trail in the spirit of the [San Francisco Crosstown Trail](https://crosstowntrail.org/crosstown-trail/) (17 miles, ~45% roads/sidewalks, 15% paved paths, 40% trails, connecting parks, staircases, and neighborhoods).

Pacifica's geography is different from SF's — it's a narrow north-south strip squeezed between the Pacific and the Santa Cruz Mountains/GGNRA ridgeline — so the natural "crosstown" axis runs **north to south along the length of the city**, weaving between the coast, neighborhood streets, and the eastern hills. This mirrors the Crosstown Trail's mix of terrain rather than copying its compass direction.

## Proposed route (north → south), ~10–12 miles

1. **Mussel Rock Park** (northern terminus, Pacifica/Daly City border) — foggy coastal bluffs, start of the trail.
2. Through **Westview / Fairmont** neighborhood streets and staircases, descending toward Sharp Park.
3. **Sharp Park / Sanchez Library area** — historic downtown core.
4. **Mori Point** (GGNRA) — coastal bluff loop trail, ocean views.
5. **Sharp Park Golf Course / Calera Creek** coastal path — part of the existing 7-mile waterfront trail.
6. **Rockaway Beach** — tourist hub, shops, pier views.
7. Climb into the **Rockaway Beach Avenue hills** (optional spur toward Mori Ridge / Sweeney Ridge for elevation and views).
8. Descend to **Pacifica Pier / Pacifica State Beach** (Linda Mar).
9. Cross **Linda Mar Valley** via neighborhood streets and parks (Pacifica's largest residential area).
10. **San Pedro Valley County Park** — wilderness trails at the valley's end, southern terminus.
11. Optional extension/spur: from San Pedro Valley Park up to **Montara Mountain – North Peak**, for a high-elevation southern bookend with sweeping coastal views.

## Why this works as a "Fog City Trail"

- Hits the defining Pacifica fog/coastal-bluff aesthetic at the north end (Mussel Rock) and, with the extension, a high-fog ridge view at the south end (Montara Mountain).
- Mixes terrain like the SF Crosstown Trail: residential streets and stairs, paved coastal path, and unpaved hill/park trails.
- Uses mostly existing infrastructure — the 7-mile waterfront trail (Mori Point → Calera Creek → Rockaway → Pacifica State Beach) forms the spine, extended north to Mussel Rock and south/east into San Pedro Valley Park.
- Total length (~10–12 mi) is a comfortable single-day hike, shorter than SF's 17 miles but proportional to Pacifica's smaller size.

## Open questions / next steps

- Walk or map the connector segments between Mussel Rock and Sharp Park, and between Rockaway and Linda Mar, to confirm walkable streets/staircases exist.
- Decide whether to include a ridge spur (Sweeney Ridge / Milagra Ridge) as an optional "high road" variant — would add significant elevation and ~3-5 miles.
- Check Pacifica's 2020 Bicycle & Pedestrian Master Plan for planned connections that could fill gaps.
- Field-check distances; current mileage is estimated from named segments, not measured.
