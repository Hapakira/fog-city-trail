The Trail

Draft route · ~10–12 miles · north to south through Pacifica, CA

Pacifica is a narrow strip squeezed between the Pacific Ocean and the ridgeline of the Santa Cruz Mountains / GGNRA — so unlike San Francisco's east-west Crosstown Trail, the natural "crosstown" axis here runs the length of the city, north to south. The Fog City Trail weaves between the coast, neighborhood streets and staircases, and the eastern hills, mixing terrain the same way the SF Crosstown Trail does.

Length and terrain

The full route is roughly 10–12 miles, hikeable in either direction in a single day. It strings together existing infrastructure — most notably Pacifica's 7-mile waterfront trail (Mori Point → Calera Creek → Rockaway Beach → Pacifica State Beach) — with neighborhood connectors at the north end and park trails at the south end. Expect a mix of paved coastal path, residential streets and stairs, and dirt park trails.

Route overview

1Mussel Rock & Westview / Fairmont

The trail begins at Mussel Rock Park, on the Pacifica/Daly City border — exposed, foggy coastal bluffs with views up the San Mateo County coastline. From here, the route drops through the Westview and Fairmont neighborhoods via streets and staircases toward Sharp Park.

2Sharp Park & Mori Point

Through Pacifica's historic downtown core near the Sanchez Library, then out to Mori Point — a GGNRA bluff-top loop with sweeping ocean views — before joining the paved coastal path along Sharp Park Golf Course and Calera Creek.

3Rockaway Beach & the hills

The coastal path arrives at Rockaway Beach, Pacifica's small tourist hub of shops, restaurants, and pier views. An optional climb up Rockaway Beach Avenue into the hills offers a "high road" variant toward Mori Ridge and Sweeney Ridge for those wanting more elevation.

4Pacifica State Beach & Linda Mar

Descending to Pacifica Pier and Pacifica State Beach, the trail then crosses Linda Mar Valley — Pacifica's largest residential neighborhood — via streets, parks, and playgrounds.

5San Pedro Valley Park

The main route ends at San Pedro Valley County Park, where neighborhood streets give way to wilderness hiking and riding trails at the head of the valley.

+Optional extension: Montara Mountain – North Peak

For a high-elevation southern bookend, continue from San Pedro Valley Park up to the North Peak of Montara Mountain — trading the coastal fog for sweeping views back over Pacifica and the ocean. This spur adds significant elevation gain and distance.
